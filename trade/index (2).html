01
Trade as Indirect Production
When Azuria specializes in Sungrain and trades, it is effectively producing Storm-Iron through an indirect route. The Magic Box is just the market — the real production happens via comparative advantage.
1 labor → 3 Sungrain → 3 Storm-Iron (via trade)
vs. 1 labor → 1 Storm-Iron (directly)
02
Comparative Advantage
Even if Azuria were better at producing both goods, it would still benefit from specializing in the good with the lower opportunity cost and trading for the rest.
Blue OC of Storm-Iron: 3 Sungrain
Red OC of Storm-Iron: 1/3 Sungrain
→ Red has comparative advantage in Storm-Iron
03
Tariff Effect
A 25% tariff doesn't eliminate trade but raises its cost. The "indirect production" route becomes less efficient — you get fewer goods per unit of labor invested in export production.
Free trade: 3 Sungrain → 3 Storm-Iron
With 25% tariff: 4 Sungrain → 3 Storm-Iron
Effective rate falls; welfare drops
04
Who Wins the Tariff?
Domestic import-competing producers gain from the tariff (less foreign competition). But consumers and export producers lose — typically more than producers gain. The tariff creates a net welfare loss.
Autarky: 6 pts → Free Trade: 12 pts → Tariff: 9 pts
Tariff recovers some gain, but not all
05
Relative Wage & Trade Viability
Comparative advantage tells us what to produce; relative wages determine whether trade is actually profitable. A country with high wages but high productivity can still export competitively — what matters is the ratio of wages to productivity, not wages alone.
Trade viable when:
1/3 < w_Blue/w_Red < 3
(= ratio of Sungrain productivities)
