What is Journalizing?

Journalizing is the process of recording business transactions in chronological order in a journal (also called the "book of original entry").

Think of it as:

  • A detailed diary of all business activities
  • The bridge between raw transaction data and organized financial records
  • The foundation for all future financial statements

Journal Organization & Best Practices

Organization Requirements:

  • Chronological Order: Record transactions by date
  • Sequential Numbering: Number each journal entry
  • Consistent Format: Same structure for all entries
  • Complete Documentation: Include all required components

For Your VVV Project:

  • Use Excel/Google Sheets for organization
  • Include columns for: Date, Entry #, Account, Debit, Credit, Description
  • Ensure total debits = total credits

Quality Control Checklist:

✓ Before Posting to Ledger:

  • All entries are in chronological order
  • Each entry balances (Debits = Credits)
  • Account names are consistent
  • Descriptions are clear and complete
  • All amounts are accurate
  • No duplicate transactions
Team Collaboration Tip:
CFO should review all journal entries before posting!

Summary & Next Steps

Key Takeaways:

  • Journalizing is the systematic recording of all business transactions
  • Every journal entry must balance (Debits = Credits)
  • Follow the DEALER rule for debit/credit application
  • Organize entries chronologically with complete documentation
  • Quality control is essential before posting to the general ledger
